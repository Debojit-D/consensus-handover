Abstract

Efficient, human-like object transfer between cooperating robots demands both spatial precision and tight temporal coordination. Prior approaches often treat these requirements in isolation or rely on pre-computed trajectories that degrade under obstacles, causing desynchronization. We present a unified, control-centric dynamical systems framework that blends asynchronous unimanual motion with coupled synchronous dual-arm coordination via two scalar gates. A shared consensus clock phase-locks translational progress and quaternion-log orientation so both arms converge to the same 6-DoF handover pose at the same instant. Translational velocities are modulated online with an obstacle-aware flow that bends paths smoothly without re-planning. We validate on dual 6-DoF industrial arms and a humanoid platform, demonstrating stable, real-time handovers that preserve spatial–temporal synchrony even when trajectories are locally deformed by obstacles.
